The Island

Ischia, amore mio

Ischia is a little slice of Southern magic, known for its beaches, thermal waters, gardens and relaxed Mediterrenean feel. It has got all the romance of the Bay of Naples, but is softer and slower.

They certainly take la vita lenta seriously... no rushing, no stress, just good food, priceless golden sunsets, and sea views that make you forget what day it is.

We hope you’ll fall a little bit in love with the island too: take your time, explore, get lost, listen to the waves, relax and fully embrace your inner isolano while you’re here with us.

Getting There & Staying

Travel Guide

Getting to Ischia

Nearest Airport

Fly to Naples International Airport (NAP).

Airport to the Port

Take the Alibus express shuttle to Naples Molo Beverello port or a taxi. The shuttle stop is directly outside arrivals.

Ferry to Ischia

High-speed ferries (Caremar or Alilauro) from Naples to Ischia Porto take 50 minutes. Book at traghetti.net. Aim for a morning crossing.

Planning Ahead

Nearest ports

Most visitors arrive into Ischia Porto, then continue across the island by taxi. Sant'Angelo is also useful for taxi boats to Maronti.

On the island

Taxis and local buses make getting around simple, and boat transfers are a lovely option for reaching Maronti Beach. You can rent a car in Ischia, but you can't take one on the ferry from Naples.

Booking early

September is a beautiful time to visit, so we recommend looking at flights and ferries as soon as you can.
